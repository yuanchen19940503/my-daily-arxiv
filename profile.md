# Research Profile (for arXiv relevance ranking)

## One-sentence summary
I work on primordial black holes (PBHs), ultralight bosons / ultralight scalar fields, gravitational waves (GWs), and black hole environments, with a focus on stochastic gravitational-wave backgrounds (SGWB), scalar-induced gravitational waves (SIGW), PBH-driven phenomenology, and ultralight-boson–black-hole systems (including “gravitational atoms” and binary-related “gravitational molecules”), especially their GW signatures across PTA/LVK/LISA bands.

## Primary research themes (high priority)
- **PBH ↔ GW connection**: GW signals and SGWB sourced by PBHs; PBH interpretations of LVK events (including mass-gap/sub-solar candidates).
- **Ultralight bosons / ultralight scalar fields ↔ BH / binary systems**: black-hole superradiance, boson clouds, black-hole–boson “gravitational atoms,” binary-black-hole–boson “gravitational molecules,” and their GW phenomenology.
- **Dynamics and constraints of ultralight-boson systems**: dynamical evolution, level transitions, environmental effects, backreaction, binary effects, and observational/theoretical constraints, especially those connected to GW observables.
- **Scalar-induced gravitational waves (SIGWs)**: modeling, spectrum features, non-Gaussian effects, and robust theoretical aspects (including gauge issues).
- **PBH formation and abundance constraints**: enhanced curvature perturbations, spectator fields, and constraints on the PBH fraction or PBH abundance from PBH-related phenomenology, e.g., high-energy photons or neutrinos emitted by PBHs, micro-lensing.
- **Observational interfaces**: forecasts and constraints relevant to LVK/PTA/LISA; connecting theoretical predictions to detector sensitivity.

## Current / near-term directions (very high priority)
- **BH or PBH environment & astrophysical processes**: GW effects from BH or PBH accretion and relativistic accretion physics; disk-driven GW signatures and related modeling.
- **PBH evolution in cosmic history**: toward more realistic PBH population evolution including mergers, accretion, and reconstruction of mass function and spin distribution.
- **Ultralight-boson–BH / binary dynamics**: formation, evolution, and GW signatures of gravitational atoms and gravitational molecules; constraints from current/future GW observations and related phenomenology.

## Secondary / lower-priority interests
- Near-horizon black hole physics or strong-gravity phenomenology not directly connected to PBHs, ultralight bosons, or GW observables.
- Broader black hole perturbation theory topics when they are not clearly linked to detectable GW signatures or to my main PBH / ultralight-boson interests.

## Methods & tools I use / value
- Analytical and semi-analytical modeling of GW spectra.
- SIGW calculations, e.g., gauge, non-Gaussianities, higher order.
- Phenomenology linking PBH scenarios to LVK/PTA/LISA observations (event interpretation, constraints, detectability).
- Modeling of ultralight-boson clouds around black holes and binaries, including superradiant growth, dynamical evolution, and GW signatures.
- Numerical / simulation-informed modeling when relevant (e.g., relativistic accretion / GRMHD-informed ingredients; planned N-body style PBH population evolution).

## What I consider highly relevant
- Any paper that provides **new PBH-related GW/SGWB mechanisms**, **new constraints**, or **new detectable predictions** (PTA/LVK/LISA).
- Any paper on **ultralight bosons / ultralight scalar fields coupled to black holes or binaries** that studies **superradiance, boson clouds, gravitational atoms, gravitational molecules, dynamical evolution, constraints, or GW signatures**.
- SIGW papers that address **formation**, **non-Gaussianity**, **gauge**, or **data analysis**.
- PBH population studies that include **mass & spin evolution**, **merger**, or **accretion**.
- Ringdown / QNM / memory-burden signatures **when tied to PBHs, ultralight bosons, or other GW-motivated black hole scenarios**.

## Usually not relevant (deprioritize)
- Modified gravity / dark energy papers unless they make concrete GW-observable predictions directly connected to my interests.
- Ultralight boson papers that are purely formal and do not connect to black holes, binaries, dynamical evolution, constraints, or GW observables.
- Generic black hole astrophysics papers without a clear connection to PBHs, ultralight bosons, or GW phenomenology.
